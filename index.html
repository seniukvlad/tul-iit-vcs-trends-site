What is a VCS?

Version Control Systems track changes to files over time, enable collaboration, and make it possible to revert or examine history. They are essential for software development and many other content-driven workflows.

Why VCS matters

  • Keeps a history of changes and authorship.
  • Supports branching and merging to manage parallel work.
  • Enables collaboration across distributed teams.
